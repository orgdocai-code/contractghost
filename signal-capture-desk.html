First response operations

ContractGhost — Signal Capture Desk

The next high-value event is not another internal document. It is the first real human response. This page turns that response into a classification, a reply path, and a logged learning in under two minutes.

Operator index Response router Spazo action card Beta intake log Request tracker

2-minute rule

  1. Paste the real reply into a scratch note or message.
  2. Classify it using the signal ladder below.
  3. Send the matching asset from response-router.html.
  4. Copy one of the logging templates below into the right markdown file.
  5. Only then decide whether to change positioning, pricing, or product.

Anti-loop rule: no strategic conclusions from a response unless it is logged first.

Signal ladder

Weak signal

  • Generic curiosity
  • Emoji / light reaction
  • No pain detail

Reply path: proof card

Log: beta intake only

Medium signal

  • Pain story in their own words
  • Trust/privacy objection
  • Asks how it works or what it catches

Reply path: proof card, sample report, or 3-clause review

Log: beta intake with objection + workaround

Strong signal

  • Needs help today
  • Sends clauses or contract
  • Mentions money, urgency, or signing deadline

Reply path: concierge review or 3-clause review

Log: beta intake + manual review tracker

What to listen for

Evidence that sharpens the wedge

  • Specific clause category repeated by multiple people
  • Same trust objection more than twice
  • Same workaround named repeatedly
  • Urgency around signing deadlines

Evidence that kills or forces a pivot

  • Target users say they rarely review contracts at all
  • They prefer a template source and never need analysis
  • Every serious lead needs paid legal advice immediately
  • No pain, no urgency, and no willingness to act after real outreach

Default next moves

Curiosity

Send proof-card.html and ask which clause type burns them most.

Privacy friction

Send three-clause-review.html instead of arguing.

Urgency

Send concierge-review.html, then log them in the request tracker immediately.
