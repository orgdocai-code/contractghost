This is a real contract analysis. The clause below is a composite of the most dangerous clauses we see in freelance contracts.

SERVICES AND DELIVERABLES 1. Contractor agrees to provide web design services as requested by Client from time to time. 2. Client may request unlimited revisions to any deliverable until Client is satisfied in Client's sole discretion. 3. All work product, including preliminary designs, concepts, and drafts, shall be considered work made for hire and shall be the exclusive property of Client upon creation, regardless of whether payment has been received. 4. Contractor warrants that all deliverables will be free from defects for a period of 12 months following delivery, and Contractor shall be solely responsible for remedying any defects at no additional cost to Client. 5. In the event that Client terminates this Agreement for any reason, Contractor shall immediately deliver all work product to Client and forfeit any unpaid compensation. 6. Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from Contractor's performance of services. 7. This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive 12-month terms unless terminated by Contractor with 90 days prior written notice.

High Risk 5 dangerous clauses found

This contract has serious issues. You'd be doing unlimited free work, giving away IP before getting paid, carrying 12-month warranty liability, losing money if the client fires you, and locked into auto-renewal. Do not sign as-is.

🔴 Unlimited Revisions Trap
HIGH RISK
Exact Quote
"Client may request unlimited revisions to any deliverable until Client is satisfied in Client's sole discretion."
What This Actually Means

The client can ask for changes forever, for any reason, and you have no legal right to say no or charge extra. "Sole discretion" means they're the only judge of when you're done — you have zero protection against this turning into 6 months of free work.

⚠️ Real consequence: We've seen freelancers trapped in 4-month revision spirals on a project that was meant to take 3 weeks. The client keeps "not being satisfied." The freelancer keeps working for free.
✅ Safer Rewrite (copy and propose this)

"Client may request up to 3 rounds of revisions per deliverable within 14 days of delivery. Additional revisions are billed at $[hourly rate]/hour."

🔴 IP Transfer Before Payment
HIGH RISK
Exact Quote
"All work product... shall be the exclusive property of Client upon creation, regardless of whether payment has been received."
What This Actually Means

The moment you create anything — a sketch, a draft, a concept — it legally belongs to the client. Even if they never pay you. You've handed over your leverage before receiving a cent.

⚠️ Real consequence: Client accepts the work, decides not to pay, and they're legally in the right. You have no IP to withhold as leverage. Your only remedy is a lawsuit — which costs more than the invoice.
✅ Safer Rewrite (copy and propose this)

"All work product shall become the exclusive property of Client upon receipt of full payment. Contractor retains ownership and all rights until payment is received in full."

🔴 Work Forfeiture on Termination
HIGH RISK
Exact Quote
"In the event that Client terminates this Agreement for any reason, Contractor shall immediately deliver all work product to Client and forfeit any unpaid compensation."
What This Actually Means

If the client fires you — for literally any reason — you hand over everything and get paid nothing for work already completed. They get the work. You get nothing.

⚠️ Real consequence: Common abuse pattern — client uses 80% of the work, terminates the contract before final payment, keeps everything under this clause. Happens in web design projects regularly.
✅ Safer Rewrite (copy and propose this)

"If Client terminates this Agreement, Client shall pay Contractor for all work completed through the termination date. Work product transfers to Client only upon receipt of all compensation owed."

🟠 12-Month Unlimited Warranty
MEDIUM RISK
Exact Quote
"Contractor warrants that all deliverables will be free from defects for a period of 12 months... Contractor shall be solely responsible for remedying any defects at no additional cost to Client."
What This Actually Means

For a full year after delivery, you're on the hook to fix anything the client considers a "defect" for free. The word "defect" isn't defined — the client could decide a design choice they approved is now a defect.

⚠️ This creates open-ended unpaid obligation. 30 days is industry standard for bug fixes. 12 months is unreasonable unless you're a software vendor selling enterprise licenses.
✅ Safer Rewrite (copy and propose this)

"Contractor warrants deliverables against defects in workmanship for 30 days following delivery. This warranty excludes defects caused by Client modifications, third-party software changes, or design decisions approved by Client."

🟠 Auto-Renewal Trap
MEDIUM RISK
Exact Quote
"This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive 12-month terms unless terminated by Contractor with 90 days prior written notice."
What This Actually Means

You're automatically locked into another 12 months of this contract — with all its bad clauses — unless you remember to send a formal written notice 90 days before the anniversary date. Miss the window by a day and you're in for another year.

⚠️ The 90-day notice requirement is unusually long for a freelance agreement. 30 days is typical. This is asymmetric — the client can likely exit more easily than you can.
✅ Safer Rewrite (copy and propose this)

"This Agreement expires at the end of the initial term unless both parties agree in writing to renew. Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice."
