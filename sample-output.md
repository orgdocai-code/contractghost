# Sample Contract Risk Summary
**Contract type:** Freelance design agreement  
**Overall risk:** Medium-High  
**Top issues found:** 5

## 1. Unlimited revisions hidden in vague scope
**Risk level:** High  
**Why it matters:** The contract says the freelancer will deliver revisions until the client is satisfied, but it does not cap the number of revision rounds. That can turn a fixed-fee project into open-ended unpaid work.

**What to negotiate:**
- Limit revisions to 2 rounds
- Define what counts as out-of-scope work
- Add a paid hourly rate for extra revisions

**Safer wording:**
"This project includes up to two rounds of revisions. Additional revisions or changes outside the agreed scope will be billed at $X/hour."

## 2. IP transfers before final payment
**Risk level:** High  
**Why it matters:** The contract transfers ownership of all work product immediately, even if the invoice is not yet fully paid. That weakens the freelancer's leverage if payment issues happen later.

**What to negotiate:**
- Delay assignment until final payment clears
- Keep portfolio display rights unless explicitly excluded

**Safer wording:**
"Intellectual property transfers to the client only upon receipt of full and final payment. Freelancer retains the right to display non-confidential work in a portfolio unless otherwise agreed in writing."

## 3. One-sided termination clause
**Risk level:** Medium  
**Why it matters:** The client can terminate at any time, but the contract does not guarantee payment for work already completed or time reserved.

**What to negotiate:**
- Require payment for completed milestones
- Add a kill fee for mid-project cancellation

**Safer wording:**
"If the client terminates the project early, the freelancer will be paid for all work completed to date plus any non-refundable reserved time or committed production costs."

## 4. Payment timing is vague
**Risk level:** Medium  
**Why it matters:** The contract says payment is due promptly but gives no specific deadline, leaving room for late payment disputes.

**What to negotiate:**
- Define invoice due dates clearly
- Add late fee language if appropriate

**Safer wording:**
"Invoices are due within 7 calendar days of issue date unless otherwise agreed in writing."

## 5. Broad indemnity obligation
**Risk level:** Medium  
**Why it matters:** The freelancer takes on broad legal responsibility for problems outside their control, which may be disproportionate for a solo operator.

**What to negotiate:**
- Narrow indemnity to actual breaches within freelancer control
- Cap liability where possible

**Safer wording:**
"Freelancer's indemnity obligations are limited to claims arising directly from freelancer's breach of this agreement or wilful misconduct."

## Bottom line
This contract is not automatically bad, but it contains several common freelancer traps that could reduce leverage, increase unpaid work, or weaken payment protection.
