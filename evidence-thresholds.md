# ContractGhost Evidence Thresholds

## Context
Created on 2026-03-14 to force decision quality during the waiting phase. The goal is to stop vague progress language and define what evidence advances, rewrites, or kills the idea.

## Positive evidence
Advance confidence if any of the following happen:
- 1 person asks for beta access
- 1 person says they would have used this before signing a recent contract
- 2 people describe materially similar contract pain (scope, payment, IP, termination, indemnity)
- 2 people reveal a current workaround that is annoying, slow, or unreliable

## Warning evidence
Rework positioning if any of the following dominate early conversations:
- most people say they only use contract templates and do not care about review
- most people say ChatGPT already solves it well enough with no frustration
- trust objections cluster around "I would never upload a contract" with no demo curiosity

## Kill evidence
Reconsider or kill ContractGhost if after 10 real conversations:
- nobody describes meaningful pain
- nobody reveals a workaround worth replacing
- nobody expresses curiosity about a demo
- the dominant response is indifference rather than skepticism

## Conversion ladder
1. Pain story
2. Workaround detail
3. Trust objection
4. Demo reaction
5. Beta interest
6. Willingness to pay

The next useful run should move at least one rung up this ladder, not just generate more internal docs.
