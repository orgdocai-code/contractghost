# ContractGhost Trust Objections

## Goal
Reduce the two biggest validation blockers:
- "Why not just use ChatGPT?"
- "I wouldn't trust AI on legal stuff"

## Positioning answer
ContractGhost is not "AI lawyer" software.
It is a **pre-sign contract risk checker for freelancers**.

The value is not just generating words. The value is:
- freelancer-specific risk patterns
- structured risk ranking
- plain-English explanations
- negotiation-ready pushback language
- fast review before signing

## Objection handling

### Objection: "Why not just use ChatGPT?"
**Answer:**
You can use ChatGPT, but most freelancers do not know what to ask, what clause types matter, or how to turn a vague answer into a negotiation response.

ContractGhost is designed to:
- look specifically for freelancer contract traps
- surface the highest-risk issues first
- explain why each clause matters
- give safer wording you can actually send back

So the wedge is not "AI exists." The wedge is **workflow + domain framing + speed under pressure**.

### Objection: "I wouldn't trust AI on legal stuff"
**Answer:**
Good. You should not blindly trust any AI on legal decisions.

ContractGhost should be framed as:
- an early warning layer
- a contract-risk explainer
- a negotiation prep tool
- not a substitute for a lawyer on high-stakes agreements

This means the trust promise is:
**"Use this to spot risk before signing, then decide whether to negotiate, ask questions, or escalate to a lawyer."**

### Objection: "My contracts are too custom"
**Answer:**
That is exactly where plain-language flagging helps. The goal is not perfect legal interpretation of every scenario. The goal is catching the obvious costly stuff people miss under time pressure.

### Objection: "$19-$29/mo still isn't worth it"
**Answer:**
Anchor against one bad contract outcome:
- one unpaid revision spiral
- one bad IP assignment
- one weak termination clause
- one missed payment term

The product is cheap insurance against a much more expensive mistake.

## Trust-building proof elements to add to landing page
1. Explicitly say it is not legal advice
2. Show the exact clause categories checked
3. Show a sample output structure
4. Frame it as "spot issues before you sign"
5. Emphasize freelancer-specific patterns

## Validation question upgrades
Instead of "Would you use this?" ask:
- What kind of contract do you sign most often?
- What term burned you hardest?
- Would you want a fast risk summary before signing?
- What would stop you from trusting a tool like this?

- First price reaction
