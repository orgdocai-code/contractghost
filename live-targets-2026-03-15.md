# ContractGhost Live Targets — 2026-03-15

## Goal
Force the next outreach run to hit real people and real pain instead of broad generic posting.

## Fresh target evidence found this run
Using direct web fetches against public search pages, the strongest visible live pain clusters still center on:
- scope creep / weak scope definition
- unpaid work / late payment after delivery
- proposal or IP misuse without payment
- general frustration with poor business practices in freelance relationships

Visible thread/post titles surfaced this run:
- "Why \"Scope of Work\" is one of your best friends in a contract"
- "First time on pushing back on scope creep with a pitch for more money!"
- "Asked a former client to finally pay for my packaging design work from July 2023..."
- "Name a better way of telling a client that expects you to do something without payment. I'll wait."
- "Client ghosted me twice… then used my whole proposal to 'build the app himself with ChatGPT'"

## Priority targets

### 1) Scope creep pain
- Channel: Reddit / r/freelance
- Search angle: `scope creep contract`, `scope of work`, `unlimited revisions`
- Why now: this is the cleanest, most repeated contract-adjacent pain and easiest for people to talk about publicly
- Ask first: "What wording do you wish you'd had in the contract before the scope started expanding?"
- Offer to share after reply: Railway page + sample risk summary

### 2) Non-payment / weak payment terms
- Channel: Reddit / r/freelance
- Search angle: `unpaid work`, `payment terms`, `client won't pay`, `late payment`
- Why now: payment language is obvious ROI; easy bridge to willingness-to-pay testing
- Ask first: "Was the problem the client being bad, or the contract/payment terms being too weak to protect you?"
- Offer to share after reply: Railway page

### 3) Proposal/IP misuse
- Channel: Reddit / r/freelance or design communities
- Search angle: `client used my work`, `proposal stolen`, `copyright`, `ownership`
- Why now: creates a sharp reason for pre-sign review and differentiates from generic ChatGPT use
- Ask first: "If a tool had flagged the ownership/payment clause before signing, would you have changed anything?"

## Suggested sequence for next outreach batch
1. One comment or DM aimed at scope creep pain
2. One comment or DM aimed at payment-term pain
3. One comment or DM aimed at IP/ownership pain
4. Log all replies or no-response outcomes immediately

## Success threshold for this target sheet
- At least one real objection or pain story captured from one of the three pain buckets above
- OR one explicit statement of current workaround (lawyer, template, ChatGPT, self-review)

## Failure interpretation
If these pain buckets still produce no signal, the issue is likely not page polish anymore. It is one of:
- wrong acquisition channel
- weak outreach timing/context
- low urgency / low-frequency use case
- trust barrier stronger than expected
