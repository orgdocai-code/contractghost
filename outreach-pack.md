# ContractGhost Outreach Pack

## Positioning decision
Lead with **"freelancer contract risk before signing"** instead of narrowly **"AI contract red-lining"**.

Why:
- Real public pain clusters around scope creep, non-payment, chargebacks, IP confusion, and one-sided termination
- "Red-lining" sounds lawyer-heavy and may reduce response rate
- The product can still do clause flagging and rewrite suggestions, but the hook should start with avoiding getting burned

## Core hook
**Don't sign a client contract blind.**

ContractGhost reviews freelancer contracts before signing and flags the stuff most likely to cost you money, rights, or leverage later.

It highlights things like:
- vague scope / unlimited revisions
- broad IP transfer
- bad payment or milestone terms
- one-sided termination
- indemnity / liability traps
- auto-renewals and hidden renewal language

Then it explains the risk in plain English and gives you safer wording to push back with.

## Fast one-liner versions
- AI contract risk check for freelancers before they sign
- Upload a client contract, get the risky bits flagged in plain English
- Spot scope, IP, payment, and termination traps before the contract bites you

## Best validation question
Instead of asking "would you use this?", ask:

**What's the clause or contract term that has burned you hardest as a freelancer?**

Then follow with:
- Did you catch it before signing or only after?
- Would you trust an AI tool to flag that before you signed?
- Would you pay $19-$29/mo for that kind of pre-sign check?
