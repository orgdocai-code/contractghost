# ContractGhost Growth Plan

## Goal
Keep distribution explicit so the project does not drift back into analysis.

## Immediate channels
### 1. Reddit validation
Priority communities:
- r/freelance
- r/copywriting
- r/graphic_design

Content angle:
- ask for the clause that burned them hardest
- lead with money leakage and contract regret, not abstract legal tech

### 2. X / LinkedIn soft outreach
Search angles:
- freelance contract
- scope creep
- client agreement
- unlimited revisions
- IP clause
- chargeback client

Method:
- reply or DM selectively
- ask one sharp question
- offer beta access, not a hard sell

### 3. Founder / consultant communities
- IndieHackers
- relevant Facebook groups
- consultant/slack communities if accessible later

## Message discipline
Lead with:
- unpaid work
- rights loss
- bad payment terms
- one-sided termination

Do not lead with:
- AI legal review
- red-lining
- compliance language

## First traction thresholds
Any of these count as real signal:
- 3 qualified beta signups
- 2 people say they would pay
- 1 user shares a real contract for review
- repeated objection cluster emerges clearly enough to improve positioning

## Objections to watch
- "I would just use ChatGPT"
- "I don't trust AI on legal stuff"
- "My contracts are too sensitive to upload"
- "I only need this occasionally, not monthly"

## Response strategy
- For ChatGPT objection: emphasize freelancer-specific risk framing and negotiation wording
- For trust objection: emphasize triage, not legal replacement
- For privacy objection: consider future local/offline mode messaging
- For occasional-use objection: test credit-based packaging later

## Next forced move after unblock
As soon as public hosting works:
1. verify URL
2. place the URL in at least 2 outreach attempts
3. capture first objection or signup
4. update state with actual response data
