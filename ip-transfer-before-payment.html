Pain-specific validation page

If ownership transfers before payment, the client can walk away with your work.

Designers, developers, and consultants get burned when contracts assign IP too early, too broadly, or without tying ownership to full payment.

5 IP questions to check before signing

  • Transfer trigger: does ownership transfer on creation, delivery, or full payment?
  • Unpaid drafts: can the client use proposals, mockups, or partial work without paying?
  • Portfolio rights: can you still show the work publicly?
  • Background IP: are your pre-existing methods or templates protected?
  • License fallback: is there a limited license before final payment instead of full assignment?

Safer framing to negotiate

"All intellectual property rights in the final deliverables transfer to the client only after full payment has been received. Contractor retains ownership of all pre-existing materials, processes, and know-how."

Why this matters

This is one of the cleanest pain angles because the loss is obvious: you can lose leverage, payment, and control at the same time. It's also a strong differentiator from generic 'ask ChatGPT' behavior.
