## Niche Research Synthesis

### What pain appears most believable
From prior founder runs and already logged workspace research, the most believable freelancer pain cluster is not “I need comprehensive legal-tech.”
It is:

1. **"I signed something sketchy because I needed the work"**
2. **"The contract was vague and now the client is stretching scope"**
3. **"Payment terms were weak and I got trapped chasing invoices"**
4. **"The IP / ownership clause was more aggressive than I realized"**
5. **"I don’t want to pay a lawyer for every small freelance contract"**

### What this means for positioning
ContractGhost should stay narrow:
- not full legal-tech
- not CLM
- not team workflow
- not contract generation first
- not broad “AI legal assistant” messaging

Instead:
- **Catch the risky bits before a freelancer signs**
- **Explain why they matter in plain English**
- **Give safer pushback wording**
- **Reduce anxiety fast**

### Likely best-fit personas
1. **Freelance designer / developer / marketer / copywriter**
   - signs client MSAs / SOWs / contractor agreements
   - worries about revision loops, payment delay, IP, termination

2. **Solo consultant / contractor**
   - gets enterprise-ish agreements from clients
   - understands business risk but not legal drafting detail

3. **Agency owner with small team**
   - signs similar documents repeatedly
   - may become a recurring buyer later

## Would Someone Pay Today?
Direct live evidence is still missing.
But the working evidence stack remains good enough to keep validating:

### Evidence in favor
- Lawyers already charge meaningful fixed-fee rates for contract review
- Templates and self-review are common because legal review is expensive relative to small freelance deal size
- A low-ticket, fast risk-check product sits between “do nothing / ChatGPT” and “pay a lawyer hundreds”
- The manual review bridge lets us test willingness to pay before fully automating

### Current pricing hypothesis
For validation, ContractGhost should optimize for **clarity + low-friction learning**, not max revenue.

Suggested pricing ladder:
- **Free:** 3-clause review / initial lightweight validation path
- **$19:** one-off founder review test for basic contract risk pass
- **$49–$79:** stronger manual review tier if people want suggested rewrite wording and quicker turnaround
- **Later recurring offer:** multi-review freelancer plan or micro-agency plan only if repeated usage appears in real requests

### Pricing benchmark logic
Even without clean web-search retrieval this run, the benchmark logic is still sound:
- human lawyers are expensive for small freelance contracts
- templates are cheap but generic
- ChatGPT is cheap but unstructured and not freelancer-specific
- a low-ticket fast review can win if it is:
  - specific
  - fast
  - trustworthy
  - honest about boundaries

### Decision
Keep using the **$19 one-off learning price** as the main willingness-to-pay probe.
Do **not** add pricing complexity until real objections / conversions justify it.

## Onboarding Flow Draft

### Goal
Turn a nervous freelancer with a contract into a clean intake without overwhelming them.

### Flow A — cold / low trust
1. Sees proof card
2. Understands risky-clause categories
3. Clicks 3-clause review
4. Pastes only the 1–3 clauses they’re worried about
5. Gets a plain-English risk pass
6. If helpful, escalates to full review

### Flow B — medium trust
1. Sees proof card or sample report
2. Understands output quality
3. Opens concierge review
4. Submits full contract or fuller context
5. Receives founder-led structured review
6. Gives feedback on usefulness + willingness to pay

### Flow C — urgent high-intent
1. Sees manual review page directly
2. Immediate answer to: “What do I send? how fast? what do I get?”
3. Sends contract / clause
4. Receives same-day manual review if possible
5. Follow-up asks what mattered most and whether they’d use it again

### Onboarding copy principles
- ask for the smallest safe next step first
- avoid legal-tech jargon
- keep boundaries explicit: not a lawyer, but useful before signing
- make turnaround expectations concrete
- tell them exactly what they’ll get back

## FAQ Draft

### Is this legal advice?
No. ContractGhost is a contract risk-checking tool for freelancers. It helps surface risky clauses fast, explains why they matter, and suggests what to question or push back on. For complex or high-stakes matters, use a lawyer.

### Why not just use ChatGPT?
You can. ContractGhost is meant to be narrower and more useful for freelancer contracts specifically: faster issue spotting, freelancer-specific risk framing, and clearer pushback language instead of a generic AI answer.

### Do I have to upload my full contract?
No. If privacy is the blocker, start with the 3-clause review path and paste only the clauses you’re most worried about.

### What kinds of clauses does it focus on?
Scope, revisions, payment terms, late fees, IP ownership, termination, indemnity/liability, and other one-sided terms that regularly hurt freelancers.

### Who is this for?
Freelancers, solo consultants, and small agencies signing client-facing agreements.

### Who is this not for?
Employment contracts, litigation-heavy documents, M&A, regulated legal matters, and anything that clearly needs formal legal advice.

### How fast is the manual review?
During validation, the target should be same day when possible. If that changes, set expectations clearly before intake.

### What do I get back?
A plain-English risk summary, the main red-flag clauses, why they matter, and safer wording or pushback angles where useful.

## Post-Response Decision Rubric
When the first real responses arrive, score them with this lens:

### Keep / sharpen if response shows
- a concrete clause pain story
- active signing urgency
- comparison against lawyer cost / ChatGPT / templates
- willingness to test the 3-clause or manual path
- useful trust objection we can design around

### Re-examine if response shows
- they care about contracts but not enough to act
- they say the problem exists but current workarounds are “good enough”
- they only want full legal advice and reject any middle-layer product

### Kill if repeated response shows
- indifference to the problem itself
- no urgency before signing
- no desire to pay even small amounts for faster confidence
- no willingness to use a middle-layer workflow even with privacy-safe options
