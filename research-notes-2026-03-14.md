# ContractGhost Research Notes — 2026-03-14

## Goal
Document parallel research completed while blocked on GitHub Pages enablement.

## Live pain evidence gathered this run

### 1) Scope creep is still a front-line freelancer pain
Source: Reddit /r/freelance search for scope creep
Observed post:
- "Lost $2,300 to scope creep on one project. How do you prevent this?"
- Details included: original 20-hour landing page engagement turned into 43 hours after added blog, color-scheme changes, and extra copy revision requests.
- The poster explicitly asked whether tighter contracts are the answer.

Why it matters:
- Confirms freelancers do not experience the problem as abstract legal review.
- They experience it as money leakage from vague scope and revision terms.
- This supports the ContractGhost positioning shift toward "pre-sign contract risk check" rather than legalistic red-lining.

### 2) Termination / project pause ambiguity is also painful
Source: Reddit /r/freelance search for contract
Observed post:
- "Client pausing project without telling me — normal for contract work?"
- The poster describes silence after deliverables, no closure note, and uncertainty over whether the work stream was ongoing.

Why it matters:
- Confirms that unclear continuation, pause, and termination language creates real confusion and stress.
- ContractGhost should explicitly flag vague pause/termination language and suggest cleaner wording.

### 3) Hourly / research-time proof is a related adjacent pain
Source: Reddit /r/copywriting search for contract
Observed post:
- "How do you verify research hours for hourly contracts?"
- Concern was not just writing work itself but proving surrounding research time to finance.

Why it matters:
- Indicates a second-wave feature possibility later: work-proof / scope logging / justification notes.
- Not MVP. Good future expansion path if contract review gains traction.

### 4) Designers are already thinking in contract clauses when AI changes delivery expectations
Source: Reddit /r/graphic_design search for contract
Observed post:
- Designer considering adding a contract stipulation refusing recreation of AI-generated images.

Why it matters:
- Confirms freelancers actively modify contracts around new risks.
- This is promising for a product that not only flags risk but suggests safer clauses freelancers can push back with.

## Synthesis
The strongest repeated pain themes remain:
1. Scope creep / vague deliverables / unlimited revisions
2. Payment ambiguity and milestone weakness
3. Termination / project pause asymmetry
4. IP / ownership overreach
5. Liability / indemnity language freelancers don't fully understand

## Product implication
The MVP should prioritize a risk summary that is organized by freelancer outcomes:
- Could cost you unpaid work
- Could delay or block payment
- Could transfer more rights than expected
- Could let the client exit asymmetrically
- Could increase your liability

This is better than organizing the output by purely legal clause categories.

## Decision
No pivot justified. The same evidence strengthens ContractGhost.
