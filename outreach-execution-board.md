- a trust objection

## Message variants

### Pain-first
I’m testing something for freelancers that flags risky contract terms before you sign — vague scope, unlimited revisions, weak payment terms, IP grabs, one-sided termination. Curious: what clause has burned you hardest?

### Workaround-first
Quick question: how do you review freelance contracts today — template, lawyer, ChatGPT, or mostly gut feel? I’m validating a freelancer-focused contract risk review and trying to understand what people actually trust.

### Trust/proof-first
I’m testing a founder-led manual contract review flow for freelancers before automating more of it. The goal is simple: catch the risky clauses early and explain them in plain English. What would you need to see to trust a tool like that?

## Logging rule
The moment any real response exists:
1. add it to `contractghost/manual-review-request-tracker.md` if it becomes a request
2. add it to `contractghost/beta-intake-log.md` if it contains meaningful language or objections
3. promote anonymized learning to `contractghost/results-ledger.html` only after the evidence is real

## Anti-loop rule

- demo asset quality
- proof capture
- intake speed
- response-time discipline
- pricing clarity
