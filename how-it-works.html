How ContractGhost works before you sign a freelance contract

ContractGhost is built for freelancers who need a fast pre-sign contract check without paying a lawyer for every small job. It reads the agreement, pulls out the clauses most likely to hurt you, explains what they mean in normal language, and gives you safer wording to negotiate with.

What problem this solves

Most freelancers do not get burned because they ignored obvious danger. They get burned because the contract language looked harmless, seemed standard, or felt too annoying to unpack before the project started. Scope creep, IP transfer before payment, weak termination rights, and vague approval language are where small gigs turn into expensive mistakes.

ContractGhost is meant to catch those issues early, while there is still time to negotiate.

The workflow

  1. Paste or upload the contract. This can be a client agreement, proposal, statement of work, or MSA.
  2. Get a risk scan. ContractGhost looks for clause patterns that commonly hurt freelancers.
  3. Read the plain-English explanation. Instead of legal jargon, it tells you what can actually happen.
  4. Use the safer rewrite. You get negotiation-ready language to send back to the client.
  5. Decide what to sign, question, or push back on.

What ContractGhost checks for

  • Unlimited revisions and vague scope language
  • Ownership transfer before full payment clears
  • Late payment risk and weak milestone wording
  • One-sided termination or refund terms
  • Broad indemnity and liability exposure
  • Auto-renewal traps and notice window gotchas
  • Non-compete, exclusivity, and confidentiality asymmetry

Why not just use ChatGPT?

You can absolutely paste a contract into a general-purpose AI tool. The problem is that general tools do not start from a freelancer-risk lens. They often summarise everything evenly instead of ranking the clauses most likely to cause unpaid work, ownership loss, or client leverage.

ContractGhost is opinionated. It is designed to prioritise freelancer pain, not just restate the contract back to you.

What the output looks like

A finished review gives you an overall risk level, the top action to take before signing, and a short list of the clauses that matter most. Each flagged clause includes:

  • The risky excerpt
  • Why it is dangerous in practice
  • A safer rewrite or negotiation angle
  • A severity level so you know what to fix first

What it is not

ContractGhost is not a law firm and does not replace a qualified lawyer. It is a fast pre-sign screening layer to help freelancers spot obvious risk, negotiate better, and know when a contract deserves a deeper legal review.

Best use cases

  • Freelance designers reviewing client project agreements
  • Developers checking SOWs and retainers for scope creep
  • Consultants negotiating ownership and payment language
  • Solo studios sanity-checking contracts before sending them to a lawyer
