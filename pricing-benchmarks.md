# ContractGhost Pricing Benchmarks

## Goal
Estimate a validation-friendly first pricing structure while blocked on public launch.

## Benchmark logic
ContractGhost is not competing with full legal review. It is competing with:
- doing nothing and hoping
- using ChatGPT manually
- paying a lawyer for each contract
- using generic contract tools that are not freelancer-risk-specific

## Market anchor points
### Human legal review
- Typical lawyer review for even a small contract is materially more expensive than a lightweight SaaS subscription.
- The relevant anchor is not exact average market pricing; it is that many freelancers perceive formal review as too expensive to use on every deal.

### Generic SaaS alternatives
- Generic document, e-sign, and contract tools exist, but they usually optimize for sending and signing contracts, not reviewing client-provided contracts from the freelancer's perspective.
- This means ContractGhost can price on pain avoided, not on seat parity with e-sign tools.

## Best validation pricing
### Recommended public beta framing
- Starter: $19/month
- Pro: $29/month
- Founding user option: lifetime deal or 3 months discounted for early feedback

### Why this still looks right
- Cheap enough to feel like insurance
- Expensive enough to test actual willingness to pay
- Clearly below the cost of one lawyer review or one scope-creep mistake

## Pricing message
Suggested positioning:
- "Cheaper than one contract mistake."
- "A tiny monthly insurance policy before you sign."

## Future packaging ideas
### Option A — subscription-first
- Starter: 5 contract checks / month
- Pro: 20 contract checks / month + negotiation wording suggestions

### Option B — credit-based
- 3 checks for $15
- 10 checks for $39
- Better if freelancers resist subscriptions

## Decision
Keep the current validation range of $19-$29/month.
Do not over-engineer pricing before the first traction signal.
