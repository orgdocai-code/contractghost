# ContractGhost First 10 Conversations Plan

## Context
Founder loop run on 2026-03-14 13:02 AEDT. Hosting is still blocked, so the highest-value move is tightening the exact path to the first 10 real validation conversations instead of rechecking the same blocker.

## Goal
Get 10 real freelancer conversations that produce at least one of:
- pain story
- current workaround
- trust objection
- willingness-to-pay signal

## Priority personas
1. Designers doing client project work
2. Copywriters / marketers on retainers
3. Developers taking custom freelance gigs
4. Virtual assistants / operators signing client service agreements

## Best trigger moments
Reach out only where the pain is already active:
- someone mentions scope creep
- someone complains about unpaid work or vague milestones
- someone mentions IP ownership or portfolio rights
- someone asks whether a contract clause is normal
- someone says they just sign templates without understanding them

## Conversation target mix
- 4 Reddit conversations
- 3 X / LinkedIn reply or DM conversations
- 3 direct warm/manual conversations via any reachable freelancer contact surface

## Core opening message
"I'm testing a tool that flags risky freelancer contract terms before you sign — things like vague scope, unlimited revisions, IP grabs, bad payment terms, and one-sided termination. If useful, I can send a quick screenshot-style demo and add you to the beta manually. What contract clause has burned you the hardest?"

## Follow-up sequence
### If they reply with a pain story
1. Ask how they review contracts today
2. Ask whether they caught the issue before or after signing
3. Ask whether they would want a fast pre-sign risk check
4. Ask whether they would pay for that if it reliably caught the risky bits

### If they reply with skepticism
1. Ask what they would need to trust a tool like this
2. Offer screenshot demo instead of asking for their real contract
3. Clarify it is risk spotting + negotiation prep, not legal advice

### If they say "I just use ChatGPT"
1. Ask what prompt they use today
2. Ask what is annoying or unreliable about that workflow
3. Position ContractGhost as structured freelancer-specific review instead of generic prompting

## Evidence capture rules
For every response, log into `contractghost/beta-intake-log.md`:
- date + source
- persona
- exact pain story summary
- current workaround
- would use
- would pay
- main objection
- next step

## Success criteria
Strong progress from the first 10 conversations looks like:
- 3+ people describe a painful contract failure or fear
- 2+ people reveal a current workaround worth replacing
- 2+ repeated trust objections cluster around the same issue
- 1+ person says they would pay or asks for access

## Kill criteria
If 10 real conversations produce no meaningful pain, no trustable signal, and no willingness to try, re-examine positioning or restart idea generation with the learnings documented.

Next useful run should do one of:
1. Verify live public URL and use it in at least 2 conversations
2. Add the first real external response to `contractghost/beta-intake-log.md`
3. If still fully blocked, prepare screenshot artifacts for manual demo delivery
