## Post 1 — r/freelance style validation post
**Title:** Freelancers: what contract clause has burned you the hardest?

I'm building a tool for freelancers that reviews client contracts and flags risky clauses in plain English before you sign.

The goal is simple: catch stuff like unlimited revisions, broad IP grabs, one-sided termination, auto-renewals, and bad payment terms without needing to pay a lawyer for every small contract.

I'm not trying to replace a lawyer. More like: "show me the dangerous bits fast so I know what to push back on."

If you've been burned before, what was the clause?
- unlimited revisions?
- IP ownership transfer?
- liability/indemnity?
- payment on vague milestones?
- client can terminate anytime but you can't?

If you want, I'll send early access when the beta is ready.

## Post 2 — IndieHackers / founder / consultant audience
**Title:** Testing a tiny SaaS idea: AI contract red-lining for freelancers

I'm validating a product called **ContractGhost**.

**Pitch:** upload a freelance contract, get:
- risky clauses flagged
- plain-English explanation of what they mean
- suggested rewrite language to negotiate back

Why I think it might matter:
- a lot of freelancers don't send contracts — they sign the client's
- legal review is too expensive for smaller projects
- ChatGPT can help a bit, but it doesn't structure the review around freelancer-specific risks

Current questions I'm trying to answer:
1. Would freelancers actually pay $19-$29/mo for this?
2. Is the killer feature the risk summary, or the suggested negotiation edits?
3. What contract clause causes the most regret after signing?

If you're a freelancer/consultant and want to roast the idea, I'd genuinely appreciate it.
