# ContractGhost FAQ

## What is ContractGhost?
ContractGhost is an AI contract risk checker for freelancers and consultants. Upload a client contract before signing and it flags risky terms in plain English.

## Is this legal advice?
No. ContractGhost helps you spot likely risks and gives negotiation ideas faster, but it is not a law firm and does not replace a qualified lawyer.

## What kinds of risks does it flag?
ContractGhost is designed to highlight freelancer-relevant risks like:
- vague scope or unlimited revisions
- weak payment or milestone terms
- broad IP transfer
- one-sided termination
- liability or indemnity traps
- hidden renewals or exclusivity terms

## Who is it for?
Freelancers, consultants, solo operators, and small agencies who often receive contracts from clients and want a fast second set of eyes before signing.

## Why not just use ChatGPT?
You can use ChatGPT, but ContractGhost is meant to structure the review around the specific ways freelancers get burned, then present the risks in a cleaner, more actionable format.

## Why would I pay for this?
Because one bad contract can cost far more than a small monthly fee through unpaid revisions, delayed payment, rights loss, or ugly termination terms.

## What file types will it support?
MVP target is PDF first, with DOCX support next.

## Does it rewrite the whole contract?
No. The MVP focus is on flagging risky clauses, explaining why they matter, and suggesting safer wording you can use to negotiate.

## Can I use it for every jurisdiction?
It can still identify many common risk patterns, but jurisdiction-specific legal advice should come from a qualified professional.

## What if I already have a lawyer?
Great. ContractGhost can still help you triage small contracts quickly and escalate only the ones that look truly risky.
